How it all began

I've always been a curious, creative person who also loves a challenge. When I was little I loved to dance (I've been a ballerina from age 4 to 9), I enjoyed drawing my favorite characters and writing down stories that lived in my mind (still do sometimes!).
I was also deeply fascinated by space, video games, puzzle games of any kind and computers.
I even owned a real telescope to watch the moon with!

I loved browsing the internet and that's how I stumbled upon a platform called fan-club.it (now only available through the Wayback Machine) on September 21st in 2012.
I was immediately intrigued by all the amazing, messy, colorful fan websites hosted on there, so I decided to create my own: rainbowdash.fan-club.it, dedicated to my favorite My Little Pony character (I was 10 years old, okay?).

Unfortunately, after a little more than a year after the creation of my website, fan-club.it closed down.
I was devastated. That silly little website had become my biggest hobby after school and I was SO happy and proud of it. I was so sad that I gave up coding altogether, but I had an amazing experience with it. I learned the basics of HTML, CSS and even a tiny bit of Javascript and Gimp. I decorated my website and interacted with the community. It was my creative outlet, but now it was all forever gone.

How it all came back

During my teenage years I was kind of a rebel but in a good way.
I kept on drawing and writing, I tried theater, I improved my English (got a B2 Cambridge Certificate when I was 16), failed at school and then got back on track all on my own and managed to graduate in time with my old classmates.
No private schools or tutoring, I locked myself in my room for a year and made it happen out of nothing but willpower.

My family was having financial problems at the time and I couldn't even afford a pc for my studying or all of the required books, but I succeeded anyway just with a smartphone, an internet connection, pen and paper.
I've always learned better independently and with a hands-on approach, which is not exactly the Italian school system way of doing things.

After high school I traveled outside of Italy for the first time in my life (I visited Copenhagen and Malmo) and went to many concerts and music festivals across Italy. I discovered a love for exploring places and nature and found joy in a simple, quiet life.

I tried studying history at Uni and it was going really well - I had amazing grades. But I just didn't like it. I figured I couldn't force myself on a path I didn't recognize as my own, so I applied for a year of Servizio Civile and got in (which I'm currently doing) to take a break and really try to understand what I want to do in life. Last summer I've also been a Tour Guide for Eventi in Bus, guiding and assisting 50+ people in the bus travel from Naples to a concert in Rome and back. It was challenging but really fun. I love adventures!

In the last few years I also tried sewing and embroidery and made a couple cute projects successfully. I loved to break clothes apart and reassemble them in a new way. I really enjoyed the challenge and creativity of it and I'm still very proud of my works.
Eventually though I gave it up because of the clutter :p for real! I'm a minimalist (both regular and digital) and I thrive when I simplify my life and my surroundings. I like to turn chaos into order.

People tell me I'm also naturally good at copywriting and selling stuff online, from my secondhand Vinted shop to curating friends' Youtube channels and Instagram pages to attract followers. I have a way of understanding what works and what doesn't with the target audience and I really enjoy selling and communicating through my writing. I'm an introvert and I thrive when I'm able to work with my brain.

For all these years, I've always had fond memories of my webmaster, coding days. So in the past few months I figured, why not start again? And this time for real! It could even become my career, who knows... working remotely and being location-independent would be my dream.

After some research, I stumbled upon The Odin Project. I have no words to describe how much I'm enjoying it. It has the perfect approach for my autonomous learning style and I'm loving the freedom to find out stuff on my own by researching, trying and doing all the challenges.
The Odin Project just gives you the right materials and then you're on your own to figure out solutions. I feel like it's really improving my problem solving skills and I look forward to keep on learning more as I go!
